Vehicle Overrun Measurement System

The determination of vehicle outline size and weight exceeding the limit is becoming more and more important to the traffic system: (1) For the judgment of vehicle size exceeding the limit, this project uses binocular vision to measure the length, width and height of the vehicle, replacing the traditional laser radar technology, with low cost, strong robustness, high precision, and high degree of visualization: using Gaussian mixture Model to solve real-time detection and tracking of vehicles; improve sparse feature point detection to enhance system robustness; evaluate and improve the performance of modules such as dual-target positioning and horizontal plane calibration to achieve high-precision vehicle size estimation. (2) For the determination of vehicle weight exceeding the limit, this project uses monocular vision to calculate the number of wheel axles and determine the single and double tires of the wheels, so as to determine the truck model and theoretical load-bearing value, with low cost and strong robustness: Hough circle transformation and Fu Lie-Merlin transformation detects wheels; uses Kalman filter to track wheel position and calculates the number of axles; combined with deep learning Resnet, classifies wheels as single tire or twin tires; research results have been applied to truck axle type recognition and overweight judgment at highway toll stations .
